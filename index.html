So You Want to Make Posts to This Blog

Dec 4th, 2012 | Comments

I decided to use Octopress for this blog since it works with Github Pages and it’s got HAML support. As it turns out, the procedure for actually making a post is pretty involved. I don’t want to the be the only one making posts about our Hack-A-Thon, so here is the procedure for making posts.

Octopress is a self-described blogging framework for hackers. Most of the folks in the BOSS meetup fit that description, so it shouldn’t be too tough. You’ll need a Github account, some rudimentary ruby skillz, and a burning desire to put something on the Interwebs. I’ll use HAML for all of the examples, but you can use Jekyll if you want.

First BOSS Hack-A-Thon Scheduled

Nov 27th, 2012 | Comments

Birmingham Open Source Software has scheduled our first Hack-A-Thon for 26-Jan-2013, also known as the last Saturday in January, 2013.

Several potential projects were suggested at our last BOSS meetup. This list will most likely grow, so make sure you check back often. Additional nominations are encouraged - just post your suggestions in the comments

We’re planning on having a virtual meeting in December to nail down the details. Date and time of the December meeting will be posted on the BOSS Meetup Page
